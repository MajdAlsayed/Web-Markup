How Food is Wasted at Home

Food waste is a big issue in homes, supermarkets, and restaurants.
Learning what causes it can help us take steps to reduce it.

Main Content

Food Waste at Home

How Food is Wasted at Home

In households, food waste happens because people often buy more than they need, don’t store food properly, or throw food away based on confusing expiration dates.

  • Overbuying: Buying too much food at once, leading to spoilage.
  • Improper Storage: Food going bad because it wasn't stored correctly.
  • Confusing Expiration Dates: Throwing away food that is still good because of misunderstandings about labels like "best before" or "use by".

Supermarket Wastage

Supermarket Wastage

Supermarkets throw away food because of overstocking, slightly damaged packaging, or high standards for how food looks.

  • Overstocking: Ordering more food than they can sell.
  • Damaged Packaging: Discarding products with minor packaging damage.
  • Aesthetic Standards: Rejecting fruits and vegetables that don't look perfect, even though they are edible.

Restaurant Food Waste

Restaurant Food Waste

Restaurants often waste food by over-preparing meals, serving large portions that customers don’t finish, and keeping ingredients for rarely ordered dishes.

  • Over-preparation: Preparing more food than needed to ensure they don't run out.
  • Large Portions: Serving portions bigger than most people can finish.
  • Rare Menu Items: Stocking ingredients for items that aren’t ordered often, leading to spoilage.
