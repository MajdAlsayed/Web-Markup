The Environmental Impact of Food Waste

Food waste contributes significantly to environmental damage. Reducing food waste can have a positive effect on the planet.

Methane Emissions Card

Food Waste and Methane Emissions

When food waste is sent to landfills, it decomposes and produces methane, a potent greenhouse gas that contributes to climate change. Reducing food waste reduces methane released into the atmosphere.

  • Methane from Landfills: Food waste in landfills is one of the largest sources of methane emissions.
  • Impact: Methane is 25 times more effective at trapping heat in the atmosphere compared to carbon dioxide.

Water, Land, and Energy Card

Wasting Water, Land, and Energy

Every time we throw away food, we are also wasting the resources used to produce it, like water, land, and energy. Minimizing food waste helps conserve these valuable resources.

  • Water: Producing food uses a tremendous amount of water. For instance, producing 1 kilogram of beef requires about 15,000 liters of water.
  • Land: About 28% of the world's agricultural land is used to grow food that is never eaten.
  • Energy: From production to transportation, food requires energy at every stage of the chain. Wasting food also wastes this energy.

Solutions Card

Solutions to Reduce the Environmental Impact of Food Waste

Reducing food waste is one of the simplest ways we can all help protect the environment.

  • Reduce Food Waste: Only buy what you need and try to prevent methane emissions.
  • Repurposing Leftovers: Get creative with leftovers by turning them into new meals to prevent waste.
  • Thoughtful Shopping: Plan your meals and only buy what you need to avoid overbuying and wasting food.
