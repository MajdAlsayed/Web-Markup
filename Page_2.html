Proper Food Storage for Fruits and Vegetables

Proper Food Storage for Fruits and Vegetables

Keep Your Fruits and Vegetables Fresh Fruits and vegetables are often wasted because they spoil quickly when not stored properly. Knowing the right place to store each type can help them last longer.

  • Leafy Greens: Store in the fridge, in a sealed container or produce bag to keep moisture in.
  • Tomatoes: Keep them at room temperature; refrigeration can change their texture.
  • Bananas: Store them at room temperature, away from other fruits to slow ripening.
  • Root Vegetables: Store in a cool, dark place (e.g., potatoes, carrots) to prevent sprouting and spoilage.

Extend the Life of Dairy and Meat Products

Extend the Life of Dairy and Meat Products

Dairy products and meats can spoil quickly if not stored at the right temperature. Keeping them in the coldest part of the fridge helps them last longer.

  • Dairy: Store milk, cheese, and yogurt in the coldest part of the fridge (not in the door, where temperature fluctuates).
  • Meat: Keep raw meat sealed and stored in the lowest part of the fridge to prevent cross-contamination and keep it cold.
  • Eggs: Store in their original carton in the fridge to keep them fresh and prevent odors from other foods affecting them.

Freeze Foods to Make Them Last Longer

Freeze Foods to Make Them Last Longer

Freezing food is one of the best ways to extend its shelf life and reduce waste. Many foods can be safely frozen and used later.

  • Bread: Freeze slices of bread to use as needed instead of letting it go stale.
  • Meat and Fish: Freeze meats and fish in airtight containers to keep them fresh for longer.
  • Leftovers: Freeze leftover meals to prevent spoilage and have ready-to-eat meals for later.
  • Fruits and Vegetables: Freeze fruits like berries and vegetables like peas to use in smoothies or soups.
